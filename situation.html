01 · The Brief

What they needed

FaithStream is Africa's first faith-based streaming platform. They came to me with a romantic comedy brief. They wanted two different directions, each strong on its own.

The Story
A woman tries to escape family pressure at a gathering. In the moment, she says she is engaged to a stranger standing right there. The problem is, the lie starts to feel real.
Rom-Com Nigerian Gen Z
The Ask
Two poster ideas with different looks. Each one had to work for both posters and billboards. It had to feel Nigerian, feel current, and tell the story in its own way.
2 Concepts Poster + OOH With Rationale

02 · Option A

The Golden Era warm, elevated, cinematic

This direction leans into something timeless. A sky that moves from deep teal to warm sand. Gold lettering. A soft sunburst behind the cast. It feels like a celebration before you read the title.

Portrait Key Art
Landscape / Banner
Billboard Mockup
01
The Colours
The teal to sand gradient feels like a Lagos morning sky. Warm and familiar. The gold comes from Yoruba celebration culture. It feels rich but still local.
02
The Typography
The large S acts as the centre of the design. Almost like a wedding monogram. The rest of the title creates contrast. It is bold and easy to remember.
03
The Composition
The sunburst gives it a sense of importance. The female lead is front and centre. You can tell she carries the story without reading anything.

03 · Option B

The Street Code bold, alive, very now

This one speaks more to a younger audience. A textured cream base, petals in motion, and a hand-drawn title. It feels warm but full of energy. The kind of poster you pause for.

The Colours

The Typography

The Rose Petals
